# Having Stock Automatically Updated by Sales

## Context

During service, staff are focused on customersâ€”not inventory.

When items are sold, the store expects stock levels to reflect that activity without requiring anyone to manually update counts after every transaction.

Automatic stock updates are about **trusting the system to keep up with reality** while people do their jobs.

---

## The Situation

Sales happen continuously throughout the day.

As items are sold:
- ingredients are used
- packaged goods leave the shelf
- retail units are handed to customers

No one has time to stop and adjust inventory for each sale.  
That work must happen in the background.

---

## What the Staff and Manager Expect

They expect:
- stock levels to change as sales happen
- no extra steps after completing a sale
- inventory views to remain meaningful during the day
- confidence that sales are reflected consistently

They do **not** expect:
- to manually deduct stock for every order
- sales to leave inventory unchanged
- stock numbers to lag behind reality without explanation

From their perspective, stock following sales should feel **automatic and reliable**.

---

## Constraints & Reality

- Not every item affects inventory in the same way
- Some materials are tracked precisely, others are not
- Sales volume can be high during peak hours

Automatic updates must work quietly, without slowing down service or requiring attention.

---

## How Modula Helps

Modula supports this situation by:
- updating stock as a natural result of selling items
- applying tracking rules consistently once they are defined
- ensuring that staff do not need to think about inventory during checkout

Inventory remains aligned with operations, without adding friction.

---

## What This Story Is Not About

- Defining what should or should not be tracked
- Adjusting stock manually
- Handling mistakes or corrections
- Explaining how updates are calculated

Those belong to other stories or system layers.

This story is about **letting sales drive inventory naturally**, so people can focus on serving customers.
